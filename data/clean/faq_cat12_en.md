# General Questions about Wage Protection System (WPS)

## 1- What is the Wage Protection System (WPS) 2.0?

The system requires employers to pay workers’ wages through [licensed banks](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/636) and [financial institutions](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/637) approved by the [Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB)](https://www.cbb.gov.bh/) and are registered with the Labour Market Regulatory Authority (LMRA) based on specific rules. The system has been developed electronically by the Labour Market Regulatory Authority (LMRA). It ensures transparency, accountability, and timely payment of wages, while enabling monitoring and enforcement of compliance with labour regulations. Employers are required to submit the payroll file via the [Expatriate Management System (EMS)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/EMS_Web/) and then approve the file through their designated banking channel to complete the salary transfer process.

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 2- What are the benefits of the Wage Protection System for both the worker and the employer?

The Wage Protection System (WPS) ensures:

- Timely payment of workers' wages.
- Prevents delays in payments.
- Reduces labour disputes.
- Protects workers' rights.
- Promotes harmony in the work place.
- Builds trust between employers and employees, and contributes to a more productive and motivated workforce.
- Enhances transparency by providing a clear record of wage payments.
- Supports compliance with labour laws.
- Simplifies auditing and reporting processes.
- Minimizes risks associated with cash handling.
- Promotes financial inclusion by encouraging workers to use bank accounts.
- Enables government authorities to monitor wages disbursement in real time.
- Strengthens ethical employment practices.
- Enhances the reputation of the employer.
- Reduces administrative burden.
- Boosts employee satisfaction and retention by ensuring fair and consistent payment practices.

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 3- Does the Wage Protection System apply to all economic sectors?

Yes, it applies to all sectors without exception.

Last Update: Tuesday 7 October 2025

## 4- Does the Wage Protection System include Bahraini and expatriates’ workers?

Yes, it includes all workers, both citizens and expatriates, in the private sector.

Last Update: Tuesday 7 October 2025

## 5- How can I transfer the salary for domestic workers?

You can transfer the salary directly through any of the [approved banks](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/636) or [payment service providers](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/637) authorized by the [Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB)](https://www.cbb.gov.bh/) and registered with the Labour Market Regulatory Authority (LMRA).

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 6- Is enrollment in the Wage Protection System mandatory?

Yes, for all employers in Bahrain’s private sector, enrollment in the Wage Protection System is mandatory under Bahraini law.

[Under Resolution (68) of 2019 (effective July 11, 2019)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/640), every private sector employer is legally required to pay employee wages through the WPS using [approved banks](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/636) or [payment service providers](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/637) authorized by the [Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB)](https://www.cbb.gov.bh/) and registered with the Labour Market Regulatory Authority (LMRA).

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 7- What are the protection measures for wage data confidentiality?

The data is stored securely according to legal regulations and labour market governance. No information is disclosed except within legally permitted limits.

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 8- What are the steps to register in the Wage Protection System (WPS)?

1. Register in the Wage Protection System (WPS) via the [Expat Management System (EMS)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/EMS_Web/) using [advanced eKey 2.0](https://www.ekey.bh/bnaf-usermgmt/pages/?14&loc=en).
2. Select (Wage Protection System) option.
3. The employer appoints himself/herself or authorizes someone on his/her behalf (Wage Responsible Person (WRP)) to register in the Wage Protection System and bears all responsibilities related to managing and monitoring the wages of the company or establishment’s workers.

> For more information, you can refer to the [Wage Protection System (WPS) Guide.](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/631)

Last Update: Wednesday 17 June 2026

## 9- What are the steps to transfer salaries through Wage Protection System?

1. The Wage Responsible Person (WRP)/Maker logs in to the [Expat Management System (EMS)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/EMS_Web/) using the [Advanced eKey 2.0](https://www.ekey.bh/bnaf-usermgmt/pages/?2&loc=en).
2. Navigate to the Wage Protection System (WPS) section.
3. Select the company or establishment profile.
4. Specifying the bank account or wallet number through which wages will be transferred, along with identifying the licensed bank or payment service provider.
5. Upload or enter the salary details for the employees, including amounts and bank account information.
6. Review and verify all salary data for accuracy.
7. Confirm the salary file for processing.
8. Once approved by the WRP/checker, proceed to transfer the salaries to employees’ bank accounts through the linked bank or payment channel.
9. Monitor the status of the salary transfer and address any discrepancies if needed.

> For more information, you can refer to the [Wage Protection System (WPS) User Manual PDF (4 MB)](https://lmra.gov.bh/files/cms/shared/wps-user-manual-eng%20(1).pdf).

Last Update: Wednesday 17 June 2026

## 12- Are there penalties for not submitting salaries via the system?

The process of paying and transferring employees’ wages is governed by the provisions of the Private Sector Labour Law No. (36) of 2012, which obliges all employers to pay wages through the approved Wage Protection System. Accordingly, failure to pay wages through the system constitutes a violation of the law and is subject to the prescribed legal measures. With regard to the second, enhanced version of the [Wage Protection System (WPS 2.0)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/631), the mandatory implementation date will be announced in the near future through the official channels of the Labour Market Regulatory Authority (LMRA). We urge all business owners to follow our official communication channels for the latest updates.

Last Update: Monday 16 February 2026

## 14- What are the responsibilities of the employer in the Wage Protection System (WPS)?

Appoint themselves or designate another person to carry out the Wage responsible person’s duties, which includes managing and submitting wage-related data for employees.

## 15- Who is the Wage Responsible Person (WRP)?

The Wage Responsible Person (WRP) is an individual assigned by the employer to be responsible for processing and reviewing all required payroll files and their data in the Wage Protection System (WPS) in addition to assigning the authorized persons.

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 16- What are the responsibilities of the wage responsible person (WRP) in the Wage Protection System (WPS)?

1. Assigning a checker and maker or appointing themselves to perform these roles.
2. Creating the company’s profile in the system.
3. Entering the establishment’s bank/wallet account details.
4. Inputting or updating employee personal information, including salary details and bank account numbers/wallet numbers.
5. Adding or deleting the establishment’s bank account or wallet information.
6. Uploading, processing and confirming the [Salary File XLS (10 KB)](https://lmra.gov.bh/files/cms/shared/wps-salary-file.xlsx).

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 17- How to assign the Wage Responsible Person (WRP) in the Wage Protection System (WPS)?

Please follow the steps below to assign the Wage Responsible Person (WRP) in the Wage Protection System (WPS):

1. Log in to the [Expat Management System (EMS)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/EMS_Web/) using the [Advanced eKey 2.0](https://www.ekey.bh/bnaf-usermgmt/pages/?2&loc=en) of the Commercial Registration (CR) responsible person and select (Assign Wage Responsible Person) function.
2. The employer fills in the WRP’s information, including ID card number, smart card type, date of birth, block number, nationality, email, and telephone number.
3. The assigned WRP will receive a notification to approve the assignment by logging into [Expat Management System (EMS)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/EMS_Web/) using their own eKey.

Last Update: Wednesday 17 June 2026

## 18- How to assign the authorized person (Checker/Maker) in the Wage Protection System?

Please follow the steps below to assign the Authorized Person (Checker/Maker) in the Wage Protection System (WPS):

1. Log in to the [Expat Management System (EMS)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/EMS_Web/) using the [Advanced eKey](https://www.ekey.bh/bnaf-usermgmt/pages/?2&loc=en) of the Commercial Registration (CR) wage responsible person and select (Add or update maker or checker) function.
2. Click on (Add or update maker or checker) then fill in the authorized person’s information, including ID card number, smart card type, date of birth, block number, nationality, email, and telephone number.
3. The assigned authorized person will receive a notification to approve the assignment by logging into [Expat Management System (EMS)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/EMS_Web/) using their own eKey.

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 19- What is the role of the authorized person (Checker/Maker) in the Wage Protection System?

### Maker Rule

1. Creating worker salary profiles.
2. Initiating salary payments.
3. Adding and updating worker receiver account details.
4. Submitting case justification.

### Checker Rule

1. Confirming the salary payments.
2. Submitting case justification.

### Role of the Person with both Maker and Checker Permissions

1. Creating worker salary profiles.
2. Initiating salary payments.
3. Confirming the salary payments.
4. Adding and updating receiver account details.
5. Submitting case justification.

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 20- How many authorized people (Checker/Maker) can be assigned to one Commercial Registration (CR)?

Each Commercial Registration (CR) can assign up to 5 individuals for each role, whether as a checker, a maker, or both checker and maker.

This means a maximum of 5 checkers, 5 makers, and 5 individuals assigned to perform both roles can be designated under one CR.

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 21- Should the assigned Wage Responsible Person (WRP)/ Checker/ Maker be a registered employee under the Commercial Registration (CR)?

No, the assigned WRP (Wage Responsible Person), checker, or maker does not need to be a registered employee under the Commercial Registration (CR).

They can be assigned to these roles regardless of whether they have an employment relationship with the Commercial Registration (CR).

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 22- Is it mandatory to assign a Checker and a Maker?

No, it is optional. The person responsible for wages can perform the roles of both the checker and the maker.

## 26- What is the definition of a Fixed Wage?

A fixed wage is the amount of money agreed upon in the employment contract, which the employee receives regularly and consistently at the end of each pay cycle (usually monthly), regardless of the actual number of hours worked or performance level. This wage is considered an essential part of the employee’s total compensation and can only be changed through a formal amendment to the employment contract or in accordance with applicable laws and regulations.

### Examples of Fixed Wage:

- The basic monthly salary specified in the employment contract.
- Housing allowance or transportation allowance, if specified as fixed amounts in the contract.
- Any fixed monthly payments made to the employee regardless of performance or working hours.

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 27- What is the definition of a Variable Wage?

A variable wage is the portion of the wage that may change from one pay period to another. It depends on factors such as overtime hours, commissions, incentives, bonuses, performance-related allowances, or any other financial benefits not defined as fixed wage in the employment contract.

### Examples of Variable Wage

- Overtime pays (extra working hours).
- Commissions based on sales or performance.
- Monthly or annual performance-based bonuses or incentives.
- Shift allowance or compensation for working during holidays.
- Any additional payments based on performance evaluations or completion of specific tasks.

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 30- Do I have to upload the payroll file every month even if there are no changes to the worker’s salary or any other data?

Yes, salary transfers must be confirmed every month regardless of changes.

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 31- Can I reuse the previous payroll file for the current month?

No, you must either upload a new file or edit and submit the current month’s payroll file.

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 32- Can I upload the payroll file before the end of the month?

You can set the transfer date as long as it falls within the allowed 14 day period. That means the system will accept the transfer if the specified date does not exceed 14 days from the actual or approved transfer date.

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 33- Can I transfer the worker’s salary after the end of the month and the beginning of a new month?

Yes, you can do that, but you must specify the month to which the salary being paid corresponds in the payroll file.

Last Update: Monday 17 November 2025

## 34- What does (Pending Confirmation) status mean for the payroll file?

## 44- What are the benefits of the Wage Protection System (WPS) for the worker?

The Wage Protection System (WPS) provides several benefits for the worker, including:

The employee must communicate with the employer to obtain the reasons. If a resolution is not reached, the employee may refer the matter to the [Ministry of Labour (MOL)](https://www.mol.gov.bh/?lang=en) for an amicable settlement, file a labour lawsuit directly, or approach the [Expat Protection Centre (EPC)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/506).

## 47- Can a worker receive their salary in more than one bank account?

Yes, a worker can have more than one bank account to receive their salary.

## 49- How will the employee's name be removed from the payroll file if the contractual relationship between the employee and the employer ends?

The employee's name will be automatically removed from the payroll file 30 days after you complete the following procedures:

- If the employee is a Bahraini or GCC national, their employment must be terminated in the [Social Insurance Organization (SIO) system](https://www.sio.gov.bh/en/).
- If the employee is an expatriate employee, their work permit must be cancelled in the [Expat Management System (EMS)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/EMS_Web/).

Last Update: Thursday 18 December 2025

## 50- Should the end-of-service bonus be transferred only through the Wage Protection System (WPS)?

No, it is possible to transfer the end-of-service bonus through the [Wage Protection System (WPS)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/631) or outside the Wage Protection System (WPS).

Last Update: Thursday 18 December 2025

## 51- If there are deductions in the salary, how should they be applied?

The Fixed Salary under “Setup worker salary profile” should be as per the employment contract without any deductions ([Including Social Insurance Organization (SIO)](https://www.sio.gov.bh/)). Fixed Salary under “Create Salary File” should include all types of deductions that the employee should have against him monthly, you can deduct the amount from the fixed salary each month when uploading the salary file, as at the moment there is no such column "deductibles" in the salary file.

Last Update: Wednesday 14 January 2026

## 52- What is the social allowance?

It is a financial amount granted to support the worker’s income, typically provided only to Bahraini workers in accordance with government regulations. This amount should match what is stated in the employment contract.

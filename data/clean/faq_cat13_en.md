# General Inquiries about Labour Inspection Directorate

## 1- What is the primary role of the Labour Inspection Directorate at the Labour Market Regulatory Authority (LMRA)?

The Labour Inspection Directorate conducts regular and unannounced inspection visits to establishments and workplaces to ensure that employers and expatriate employees comply with the provisions of the [Labour Market Regulation Law](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/5), enforcing compliance and detecting violations, such as irregular employment or working without a work permit.

Last Update: Sunday 14 June 2026

## 2- How can an employer or citizen report illegal workers?

Violations (complaints) are reported via the [Notification of an Offence Service.](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/586)

Last Update: Sunday 14 June 2026

## 3- What procedures will be taken when a worker is found working without a valid work permit (Illegal Worker)?

A record and a question minute of the illegal worker will be issued, and all legal procedures will be taken to refer the case to the [Public Prosecution](https://www.ppb.gov.bh/en/).

Last Update: Sunday 14 June 2026

## 4- Do LMRA inspectors have the authority to enter establishments and workplaces at any time?

Yes, The inspectors of the Labour Market Regulatory Authority (LMRA) hold the status of judicial enforcement officers. They are legally authorized to enter work premises and branches to conduct inspections and peruse records and documents to verify the legality of employment statuses

Last Update: Sunday 14 June 2026

## 5- How can an establishment comply and avoid violations during an inspection visit?

The establishment must maintain employee records and valid work permits at the workplace. It must also ensure that all workers are registered under the same establishment or one of its branches having the same activity, and they are working in the same occupations on their work permits.

Last Update: Sunday 14 June 2026

## 6- What are the consequences if an inspector determines that the establishment is “closed” or “Inactive” during an inspection visit?

The employer is required to book an appointment for the [Remote Virtual Inspection Service (RVI)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/511) through the Inspection E-Services to verify that the establishment is active.

Last Update: Wednesday 17 June 2026

## 7- What is the procedure if an establishment found to be “active but not ready to practice the activity“ or ready but “No Activity is Practiced”?

The employer is required to book an appointment for the [Remote Virtual Inspection Service (RVI)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/627) to prove the establishment's readiness and compliance with the requirements.

Last Update: Wednesday 17 June 2026

## 8- What are the procedures followed in the event of detecting a discrepancy between the actual number of expatriate employees at the site and the number registered in LMRA’s records?

The employer is required to book an appointment for the [Remote Virtual Inspection Service (RVI)](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/627) via the Inspection E-Services to verify workforce compliance.

Last Update: Wednesday 17 June 2026

## 9- What legal action is taken if an employer is not cooperating with the LMRA inspector (judicial officer)?

Failure to cooperate with LMRA inspectors or refusal to provide the required documents and information constitutes a violation of Article 34 (b). The offender is subject to a fine ranging from BHD 500 to BHD 2,000.

Last Update: Sunday 14 June 2026

## 10- What service is available for inquiries regarding administrative observations or legal violations issued against establishments or expatriate employees by the Labour Inspection Directorate?

The [Inquiry Service](https://www.lmra.gov.bh/en/page/show/585) allows employers and expatriate employees to review administrative notices and actions, guiding them to the right e-services to complete their transactions.

Last Update: Wednesday 17 June 2026
